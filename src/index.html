Paint

Our paint team is skilled in removing unwanted objects and elements from live-action footage, fixing continuity errors, and adding or removing textures. Our paint services are often used for compositing and clean-up work.

Prep

Our prep team is responsible for preparing footage for compositing. This involves cleaning up and enhancing the footage, including color correction, removing unwanted elements, and stabilizing footage.

Roto

Roto, short for rotoscoping, is the process of creating a matte or mask around an object in live-action footage. Our roto team is skilled in creating precise, detailed mattes to allow for accurate compositing of VFX elements into live-action footage.

Matchmove

Matchmoving involves tracking the movement of live-action footage to allow for the accurate placement of VFX elements. Our matchmove team is experienced in using both 2D and 3D tracking methods to ensure seamless integration of VFX into live-action footage.

Rotomation

Rotomation is the process of capturing the movement of a physical object and applying it to a digital model. Our rotomation team is skilled in capturing precise movement data, which can be used to create realistic animations of digital characters and objects.

Data Annotation

Data annotation involves the process of labeling or tagging data to make it usable for training machine learning models. Annotated data provides a reference or ground truth for the model to learn from. Here are some common types of data annotation:

Image Annotation
Involves labeling objects, regions, or features within an image, such as object detection bounding boxes, image segmentation masks, or keypoints.

Text Annotation
Involves marking up text data with annotations like named entity recognition (NER), part-of-speech (POS) tagging, sentiment analysis labels, or document classification categories.

Audio Annotation:
Involves transcribing speech, identifying speakers, or labeling specific events or sounds within an audio recording.

Video Annotation:
Similar to image annotation, video annotation involves labeling objects, regions, or actions within a video sequence, such as object tracking, activity recognition, or action detection.

Data annotation is typically performed by human annotators, who follow specific guidelines or instructions provided by the project or task requirements. There are also tools available that assist in the annotation process, such as labeling software or platforms.
